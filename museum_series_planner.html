Museum Series Planner


I created five illustrations for Faber-Castell (Guangzhou) on this project, including one for the cover and four for the separator pages. Each one represents a significant national treasure in Chinese history. The inspiration of the one on the cover comes from the Tri-colored Glazed Pottery Camel with Musicians. It is known as one of the best tri-colored glazed potteries left over from the Tang Dynasty (from 618 to 906 A.D.). A band of musicians were sculptured vividly sitting on a singing camel. They looked so involved in the music they were playing.

The other four treasures are the Human-head-shaped Mouth Colored Pottery Bottle from the Neolithic period, the Fuhao Owl-shaped Bronze Wine Goblet from the Shang Dynasty (from 1600 to 1046 B.C.), the Changxin Palace Lantern from the Han Dynasty (from 206 B.C. to 220 A.D.), and the Cloisonné Enamel Dragon Flower Pattern Melon-shaped Lidded Jar from the Qing Dynasty (from 1644 to 1911 A.D.).
